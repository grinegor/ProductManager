## Extracted Notes

Interview-style article with app marketing operator Steve P. Young on subscription monetization tactics.

### Clean takeaways

- Show the paywall to most users (high paywall exposure) instead of letting many users never see monetization entry points.
- Put paywall/testing early in onboarding, because early-session intent can be strong.
- Do not over-invest in new features before fixing onboarding-to-trial and trial-to-paid conversion.
- Shift messaging from feature-led copy to value-led copy (outcome/benefit framing).
- Trial strategy in this interview:
  - short trials (for example 3-7 days) are often easier to monetize than long trials;
  - monitor cancellation behavior and post-trial conversion by cohort.
- Suggested benchmark ranges shared in the article should be treated as directional, not universal:
  - trial starts as a share of users,
  - paywall-to-trial activation rate.
- Hard paywalls can validate willingness to pay quickly for certain niches, but should be tested carefully against retention and user trust.
- Multi-option pricing tables can influence plan mix and increase higher-value plan selection (decoy effect patterns).

### PM-useful patterns for our agent

- Prioritize the monetization funnel order:
  1) paywall exposure,
  2) paywall conversion,
  3) trial-to-paid,
  4) early retention/refunds.
- Use experimentation with clear guardrails (refund rate, negative reviews, churn) when testing hard paywalls or aggressive pricing surfaces.
- For D2C health subscriptions, convert claims to measurable decision rules:
  - if paywall exposure < target, adjust onboarding placement;
  - if trial activation rises but paid conversion drops, revisit trial length and expectation setting.
