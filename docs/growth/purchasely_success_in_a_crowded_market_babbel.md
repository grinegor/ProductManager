## Extracted Notes

Interview with Babbel Live product leader Belen Caeiro on launching and monetizing a live language-learning subscription in a crowded market.

### Clean takeaways

- Babbel Live reportedly went through three major attempts before finding fit:
  1) building too much in-house infrastructure,
  2) private tutoring proposition with weaker scaling,
  3) group classes + off-the-shelf infrastructure that improved economics and speed.
- A recurring theme is to avoid overbuilding before validating willingness to pay.
- Team intentionally tracked core validation questions early:
  - Can we build it?
  - Do users want it?
  - Will users pay for it?
- Pricing-page simplification was treated as a key conversion lever. They reduced decision complexity and emphasized clearer commitment choices.
- View on trials from the interview: free trials may underperform for premium products where value realization takes longer than a short trial window.
- Growth approach discussed: diversify acquisition mix and increase product-led growth to reduce overdependence on increasingly expensive paid channels.

### PM-useful patterns for our agent

- In crowded markets, differentiation can come from modality shift (self-serve -> live/group experience), not only messaging.
- For subscription onboarding, reducing cognitive load at pricing can improve checkout progression.
- For premium offers, evaluate trial policy by time-to-value and cancellation behavior rather than defaulting to free trial.
- Instrumentation should exist from day one even with a small team (analyst/research support early).
