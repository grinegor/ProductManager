## Extracted Notes

Interview with Halle Tecco on market evolution in digital health.

### Clean takeaways

- Consumer-facing health models became mainstream despite early skepticism.
- Digital health maturity improved: more durable businesses and clearer examples of scale.
- Systemic constraints remain strong:
  - policy and reimbursement structures,
  - incumbent incentives,
  - slow institutional change versus consumer behavior.
- Persistent representation gaps remain, especially in leadership and capital allocation.
- Practical strategy lens: startup innovation alone is insufficient for some healthcare problems; policy and ecosystem changes are often required.
- Forward view:
  - optimism around prevention, earlier intervention, and AI/data-enabled care models,
  - concern that short-term financial incentives can undermine long-term health outcomes.

### PM-useful patterns for our agent

- Distinguish consumer demand signals from healthcare system readiness.
- For health subscription strategy, model both:
  - user-side adoption velocity,
  - system-side friction (regulation, reimbursement, clinical workflow).
- Treat distribution and trust as long-cycle assets, not only funnel optimization problems.
